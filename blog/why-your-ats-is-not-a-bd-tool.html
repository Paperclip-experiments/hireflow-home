Why Your ATS Is Not a BD Tool (And What to Use Instead)

TLDR: 23% of staffing agencies say finding new clients is their #1 challenge in 2026, up from 16% in 2024. Yet most agencies rely on their ATS — a tool built for candidate management — to handle business development. An ATS manages candidates. To manage clients, you need a dedicated BD platform with AI prospecting, outreach automation, and pipeline management.

The staffing industry's biggest challenge isn't candidates — it's clients

For the first time in years, staffing agencies are reporting that finding new clients is harder than finding candidates. According to industry data, 23% of agencies now cite client acquisition as their top operational challenge, a significant jump from 16% just two years ago.

This shift is driven by several factors:

  • Market consolidation: Larger agencies are winning more enterprise contracts, squeezing SMB agencies out
  • DIY recruiting tools: Companies have more options to recruit directly (LinkedIn Recruiter, Indeed Hire)
  • Economic uncertainty: Hiring freezes and budget cuts mean fewer active staffing engagements
  • Increased competition: More agencies compete for the same clients in popular verticals
23% of staffing agencies say finding new clients is their #1 challenge (up from 16% in 2024)

Why your ATS can't solve this problem

Tools like Bullhorn, Zoho Recruit, Manatal, and JobAdder are excellent at what they were designed for: tracking candidates through the placement pipeline. They manage resumes, job orders, submissions, interviews, and placements.

But here's what they were never built to do:

  • Identify companies with hiring intent before they contact you
  • Automate outbound BD sequences across email, LinkedIn, and phone
  • Score and rank prospects by likelihood to convert
  • Manage a client pipeline with deal stages, revenue forecasting, and activity tracking
  • Provide branded client portals for candidate submissions and status tracking

An ATS is a candidate management system. Using it for business development is like using a spreadsheet for CRM — technically possible, but you're fighting the tool instead of leveraging it.

The gap in the staffing tech stack

The staffing industry has invested heavily in candidate-side tooling over the past decade. AI-powered sourcing, automated screening, one-click job posting — the candidate experience is increasingly automated and optimized.

But the client acquisition side remains almost entirely manual. Most agencies still rely on:

  • Cold calling from purchased contact lists
  • Manual LinkedIn outreach by individual BD reps
  • Networking events and referrals
  • Generic sales tools (Apollo, Outreach) that aren't staffing-specific

The result: agencies that want to grow can only do so by hiring more BD reps, not by working smarter. The BD process doesn't scale because there's no automation, no intelligence, and no system.

What modern agencies need instead

A purpose-built BD platform for staffing agencies should combine four capabilities:

1. AI-powered prospect discovery

Instead of cold-calling from stale lists, an AI prospecting engine monitors job boards, funding announcements, and company growth signals to surface companies that are actively hiring right now. Reaching a company 3 days after they post 5 engineering roles isn't luck — it's a system.

2. Automated outreach sequences

Multi-channel sequences (email, LinkedIn, calls) with staffing-specific templates and AI personalization. Each message references the prospect's actual hiring activity, industry context, and pain points — not generic "we can help you hire" pitches.

3. Client pipeline management

A proper pipeline with deal stages (Prospect → Contacted → Meeting → Proposal → Won), revenue forecasting, activity logging, and analytics. Every BD rep should know exactly where every deal stands and what action to take next.

4. Branded client portals

Only 18% of staffing agencies offer client portals — despite clients consistently ranking communication and transparency as top priorities. A branded portal where clients can view submitted candidates, provide feedback, and track placement progress is a powerful differentiator.

3.5x revenue growth reported by staffing agencies using AI-powered BD tools (Bullhorn data)

The bottom line

Your ATS is essential — but it solves the wrong half of the equation. In 2026, the agencies growing fastest are the ones that treat client acquisition as a data-driven, automated revenue motion rather than a manual, relationship-only process.

If 23% of agencies say finding clients is their #1 challenge, the solution isn't to cold-call harder. It's to invest in purpose-built BD tooling that gives your team the same AI-powered advantage on the client side that your ATS gives you on the candidate side.
